\section{Data description}\label{sec:data-description}

We consider hourly observations from the network of automatic
weather stations operated by the Royal Netherlands Meteorological
Institute (KNMI). The empirical analysis uses one variable only,
the hourly maximum wind gust \(\mathrm{FX}\), defined as the
maximum gust speed (in metres per second) recorded by the
station in the hour preceding the timestamp. The remainder of this
section documents the source files, the variable definitions, the
station network, the missingness structure, the seasonal split, and
the analysis sample.

\subsection{Source and file structure}\label{sec:data-source}

The hourly observations used in this thesis are stored locally in
the directory
\texttt{data/yearly\_aggregated\_FH\_FX/}, with one parquet file
per (year, month) combination at the path
\begin{center}
\texttt{data/yearly\_aggregated\_FH\_FX/year=YYYY/month=MM/knmi\_fh\_YYYY\_MM.parquet}.
\end{center}
Each parquet file is a long-format table whose rows are
(\texttt{time}, \texttt{station}) hour-station observations. The
schema is the same for every month: \texttt{time, station,
stationname, lat, lon, height, FH, FX}. Of these, only
\(\mathrm{FX}\) enters the dependence estimators of
Section~\ref{sec:methodology}; the hourly mean wind speed
\(\mathrm{FH}\) is read alongside but is not the thesis variable,
and the metadata columns are used only for filtering and for
distance computation. The list of all available KNMI variables in
the underlying 10-minute archive is recorded in
Appendix~\ref{app:knmi-vars} for completeness; only the wind-gust
column is used here.

\subsection{Primary variable and units}\label{sec:data-variable}

The primary variable is

\begin{quote}
\(\mathrm{FX}\): hourly maximum wind gust, in m/s,
representative for a reference height of 10\,m above the surface.
\end{quote}

Throughout the thesis we write \(X_{i,t}\) (equivalently
\(X_{s_i,t}\)) for the value of \(\mathrm{FX}\) at station \(s_i\)
in hour \(t\). The time index \(t = 1,\dots,T\) is a discrete
hourly index. The spatial location \(s_i \in \mathbb{R}^2\) is the
WGS84 latitude/longitude pair of station \(i\); the network
contains \(N\) stations, with \(i, j = 1, \dots, N\) reserved for
station indices throughout the thesis.

The mean wind speed column \(\mathrm{FH}\) is also reported at the
hourly level and at the 10\,m reference height. It is recorded only
to keep the parquet schema stable across files; it is not used in
the empirical analysis.

\subsection{Station network and metadata}\label{sec:data-stations}

The station metadata table is stored at
\texttt{data/station\_metadata.csv} and contains, for each station,
the columns \texttt{station\_id, stationname, lat, lon, height}.
Stations are identified throughout this thesis by their numeric
\texttt{station\_id}; the name is used only for figure legends and
for the station-list appendix. The full inventory of stations is
provided in Appendix~\ref{app:knmi-stations}.

Figure~\ref{fig:knmi-stations} displays the locations of the
stations on the Dutch land area. The mapped network covers
coastal, inland and offshore positions. The empirical analysis is
restricted to Dutch land and coastal stations
(Section~\ref{sec:emp-stations}); offshore platforms in the North
Sea are recorded in the metadata file but are excluded from the
main analysis on the grounds of differing measurement environment
and intermittent record availability.

\subsection{Sample period and changing
network}\label{sec:data-period}

The local archive contains hourly partitions for 1951--2026 (911 of
912 expected (year, month) partitions are present; the June 2003
partition is missing on disk).

station network has changed substantially over this period:
several stations have been opened or closed, and a number of
historic stations have been relocated, renamed or replaced by an
automated successor. We treat the resulting panel as
\emph{unbalanced} and address this directly through the station-
inclusion rules of Section~\ref{sec:emp-stations}. In particular,
the headline empirical analysis uses a balanced subset of stations
that are present in every winter and every summer of the chosen
sample window; the larger unbalanced panel is used only as a
robustness check (Section~\ref{sec:rob-stations}).

\subsection{Missingness and quality
control}\label{sec:data-missing}

The hourly archive inherits the missingness and quality-control
structure of the underlying 10-minute KNMI product. Two types of
missingness are relevant:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{Hourly missingness.} An individual hour may have no
          recorded \(\mathrm{FX}\) value, either because the
          station was inactive or because the 10-minute
          observations from which the hourly maximum is derived
          failed quality control.
    \item \emph{Station-year missingness.} A station may be
          inactive for an entire winter or summer, in which case
          all hours in that station-season-year are missing.
\end{enumerate}

Both forms are addressed by the missingness rules in
Section~\ref{sec:emp-missing}: a minimum within-season completeness
threshold for station-year inclusion, and a maximum within-block
missing-fraction threshold for block-maxima inclusion. Pairwise
estimators of dependence are computed on pairwise-complete hours,
so a missing observation at one station does not invalidate the
pair at hours where both stations report.

\subsection{Seasonal definitions}\label{sec:data-seasons}

We use the standard climatological seasons restricted to winter
and summer:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Winter \(c = \mathrm{W}\): December, January and February
          (DJF).
    \item Summer \(c = \mathrm{S}\): June, July and August (JJA).
\end{itemize}

December of calendar year \(y\) is attached to winter year
\(y+1\); so winter 1967 consists of December 1966, January 1967
and February 1967. This convention keeps each winter contiguous in
calendar time and is implemented in
\texttt{python\_project/src/seasons.py}. Spring (MAM) and autumn
(SON) hours are dropped from the main analysis.

\subsection{Analysis sample}\label{sec:data-sample}

The construction of the analysis sample is summarised here and made
operational in Section~\ref{sec:empirical-design}. The steps are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Restrict to Dutch land and coastal KNMI stations whose
          sensor is documented as representative for the 10\,m
          reference height.
    \item Keep only hours where \(\mathrm{FX}\) is reported and is
          not flagged as invalid by KNMI quality control.
    \item Discard station-season-years that fail the within-season
          completeness threshold of
          Section~\ref{sec:emp-missing}.
    \item Restrict to the balanced station panel: stations present
          in every winter and every summer of the chosen sample
          window.
    \item Stratify the resulting panel by season into a winter
          subsample \(\{X_{i,t}: t \in \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{W}}\}\)
          and a summer subsample
          \(\{X_{i,t}: t \in \mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{S}}\}\).
\end{enumerate}
