\section{Simulation Study}\label{sec:simulation}

This chapter evaluates the three estimators under known data-generating processes. The simulation is not a proof that any model is correct for the KNMI data; it measures whether each fitted method recovers the true tail-dependence contrast \(\Delta^{\chi}(d)\) under controlled settings on the observed station geometry. Data are generated from each model class, every method is fitted to every dataset, and recovery is scored on the common curve \(\chi(d)\). Nothing here is a completed result: each quantity is one the study is designed to report, and the target values of \(R\), \(B\), and \(R_{\mathrm{boot}}\) are computational targets whose realised values are reported with the results.

\subsection{Design and notation}\label{sec:sim-design}

All simulations use the observed station set \(\{s_i\}_{i=1}^N\), \(N=33\), and its distance matrix \([d_{ij}]\), so the number of pairs per fit matches the application,
\begin{equation}
  |\mathcal P|=\tbinom{N}{2}=\tfrac{33\cdot32}{2}=528,\qquad d_{\max}\approx327\ \text{km},
\end{equation}
and the temporal layout mirrors the data, \(n_y\) season-years of \(m_c\) daily fields per season, the season-year being the bootstrap block of Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-inference}. Writing \(\mathcal M=\{\mathrm{BR},\mathrm{ExtT},\mathrm{tCop}\}\), each simulated dataset is generated by a DGP \(D\in\mathcal M\) and analysed by a fitted method \(M\in\mathcal M\), so every dataset occupies a cell
\begin{equation}
  (D,M)\in\mathcal M\times\mathcal M,
\end{equation}
with the diagonal \(D=M\) measuring correct-specification performance and the off-diagonal \(D\ne M\) measuring misspecification robustness, the generic empirical situation since at most one model can be correct.

A DGP \(D\) induces, in closed form from its parameters, a true curve per season and a true contrast,
\begin{equation}
  \chi_{0,D,c}(d),
  \qquad
  \Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)=\chi_{0,D,\mathrm W}(d)-\chi_{0,D,\mathrm S}(d),
  \qquad c\in\{\mathrm W,\mathrm S\},
\end{equation}
and in replication \(r\) the fitted method \(M\) returns
\begin{equation}
  \widehat\chi_{M,c,r}^{(D)}(d),
  \qquad
  \widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)=\widehat\chi_{M,\mathrm W,r}^{(D)}(d)-\widehat\chi_{M,\mathrm S,r}^{(D)}(d),
\end{equation}
evaluated on the grid \(\mathcal D=\{d_1,\dots,d_G\}\) and summarised at \(\mathcal D_0=\{50,100,200,300\}\)~km, identical to Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-chi}. The two scenarios are carried in the parameters of \(D\) rather than in the symbols above, and are stated in Sect.~\ref{sec:sim-scenarios}.

The target is the curve, not the range parameter. Off the diagonal the fitted model has no true parameter, and \(\rho\) is not the same object across \(\mathcal M\), so a parameter error is undefined and incomparable there; the curve \(\chi_{0,D,c}(d)\) exists for every \(D\) and is reproduced by every \(M\), so it is the only target that scores all nine cells. Parameter recovery \((\rho,\alpha,\nu)\) is reported only as a secondary diagnostic on the diagonal (Sect.~\ref{sec:sim-metrics}). Fitting three models, two of them with a three-parameter multi-start optimisation, to every dataset --- and inside every bootstrap resample --- is costly, so following \citet{morris2019} the counts in Table~\ref{tab:sim-settings} are intended targets: the main experiment of point estimation, ordering recovery, and misspecification robustness is run at the full \(R\) for all methods, while bootstrap coverage (Sect.~\ref{sec:sim-boot}) is assessed on a smaller subset, and the more expensive copula DGP is run at a reduced replication count rather than dropped.

\subsection{Data-generating processes}\label{sec:sim-dgp}

Fields are independent replicates of the spatial process on the native scale; the margins are known, so no GEV step is applied and each fitted method receives the scale it expects (Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-marginals}). The Brown--Resnick DGP \citep{brown1977,kabluchko2009} simulates on unit Fr\'echet from the variogram \(\gamma_c(d)=(d/\rho_c)^{\alpha_c}\) by the exact extremal-functions algorithm \citep{dombry2016}, with induced curve \(\chi_{0,\mathrm{BR},c}(d)=2-2\Phi\{\tfrac12\sqrt{\gamma_c(d)}\}\), which decays to zero at long range. The extremal-\(t\) DGP \citep{opitz2013} simulates on unit Fr\'echet from \(r_c(d)=\exp\{-(d/\rho_c)^{\alpha_c}\}\) with tail \(\nu_c\), again exactly \citep{dombry2016,thibaud2015}, with induced curve \(\chi_{0,\mathrm{ExtT},c}(d)=2-2T_{\nu_c+1}[\{(\nu_c+1)(1-r_c(d))/(1+r_c(d))\}^{1/2}]\) and a positive long-range floor. The spatial Student-\(t\) copula DGP simulates on the uniform scale by drawing from a multivariate \(t\) with correlation matrix \([r_c(d_{ij})]\) and applying the \(t\) probability integral transform, with induced curve as in \eqref{eq:meth-tcop-chi}; because the Student-\(t\) copula is one of the three core methods, this DGP is retained and, if its cost is prohibitive, run at a reduced replication count rather than treated as optional.

For each DGP the two scenarios are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sim-hyp}
  H_0^{\mathrm{sim}}:\ \Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)=0\ \ \forall d\in\mathcal D,
  \qquad
  H_1^{\mathrm{sim}}:\ \Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)>0\ \ \text{for relevant } d\in\mathcal D.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Fitted estimators}\label{sec:sim-est}

In every cell all three methods are fitted to each simulated dataset by the matched pairwise objectives of Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-cl} --- pairwise composite likelihood for \(\mathrm{BR}\) and \(\mathrm{ExtT}\) on \(\widehat Z\), pairwise copula likelihood for \(\mathrm{tCop}\) on \(\widehat U\) --- using the same multi-start L-BFGS-B optimiser, parameter boxes \(\Theta_M\), and per-coordinate convergence and boundary recording. Each replication \(r\) yields, for each \(M\),
\begin{equation}
  \widehat\vartheta_{M,c,r}^{(D)},\qquad
  \widehat\chi_{M,c,r}^{(D)}(d)=\chi_M\!\bigl(d;\widehat\vartheta_{M,c,r}^{(D)}\bigr),\qquad
  \widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)=\widehat\chi_{M,\mathrm W,r}^{(D)}(d)-\widehat\chi_{M,\mathrm S,r}^{(D)}(d),
\end{equation}
stored on \(\mathcal D\) with convergence status, boundary flags, computation time, and, on the diagonal only, the parameter estimates. This experiment carries no bootstrap and runs at the full \(R\); it is the primary comparison.

\subsection{Performance metrics}\label{sec:sim-metrics}

All headline metrics are on the contrast and are computed under each scenario separately; for a cell \((D,M)\) with replicates \(\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)\), \(r=1,\dots,R\), and true contrast \(\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)\), the bias and root-mean-squared error at \(d\in\mathcal D_0\) are
\begin{align}
  \operatorname{Bias}_{D,M}(d)
   & =\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\Bigl[\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)-\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)\Bigr],
  \label{eq:sim-bias}                                                                                                   \\
  \operatorname{RMSE}_{D,M}(d)
   & =\Biggl\{\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\Bigl[\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)-\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)\Bigr]^2\Biggr\}^{1/2},
  \label{eq:sim-rmse}
\end{align}
and the integrated and mean integrated squared error over the grid are
\begin{align}
  \operatorname{ISE}_{D,M,r}
   & =\frac1G\sum_{g=1}^G\Bigl[\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d_g)-\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d_g)\Bigr]^2,
  \label{eq:sim-ise}                                                                                  \\
  \operatorname{MISE}_{D,M}
   & =\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\operatorname{ISE}_{D,M,r}.
  \label{eq:sim-mise}
\end{align}
The seasonal question is directional, so under \(H_1^{\mathrm{sim}}\) the ordering-recovery rate and under \(H_0^{\mathrm{sim}}\) the spurious-ordering rate are
\begin{align}
  \operatorname{OR}_{D,M}(d)
   & =\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\mathbf 1\bigl\{\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)>0\bigr\}
  \quad(\text{under } H_1^{\mathrm{sim}}),
  \label{eq:sim-or}                                                                                        \\
  \operatorname{FP}_{D,M}(d;\varepsilon)
   & =\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\mathbf 1\bigl\{\lvert\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d)\rvert>\varepsilon\bigr\}
  \quad(\text{under } H_0^{\mathrm{sim}}).
  \label{eq:sim-fp}
\end{align}
The tolerance form \eqref{eq:sim-fp} is the planned default; where the bootstrap subset of Sect.~\ref{sec:sim-boot} is run, the interval-exclusion rate --- the share of replications whose \(95\%\) interval for \(\Delta_M^{\chi}(d)\) excludes zero --- is reported in its place, giving the false-positive rate under \(H_0^{\mathrm{sim}}\) and power under \(H_1^{\mathrm{sim}}\) without a tolerance.

The operational diagnostics are the convergence rate and the boundary-hit rate,
\begin{align}
  \operatorname{Conv}_{D,M}
   & =\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\mathbf 1\{\text{converged}\},
  \label{eq:sim-conv}                                                                       \\
  \operatorname{Bound}_{D,M}
   & =\frac1R\sum_{r=1}^R\mathbf 1\bigl\{\widehat\vartheta_{M,r}\in\partial\Theta_M\bigr\},
  \label{eq:sim-bound}
\end{align}
with \(\operatorname{Bound}\) reported per coordinate (\(\rho,\alpha,\nu\); a \(\nu\) at its upper edge flags a weakly identified tail), alongside the mean computation time per fit. A method that recovers the contrast only with frequent non-convergence or boundary pile-up is less useful than one slightly less accurate but dependable. Where the fitted model matches the DGP a true \(\vartheta\) exists, and the bias and RMSE \eqref{eq:sim-bias}--\eqref{eq:sim-rmse} are also applied to \(\rho_c,\alpha_c\), and \(\nu_c\) as a secondary diagnostic of identifiability, never as the cross-method object; the same definitions may be applied to the curve levels \(\chi_{M,c}(d)\) against \(\chi_{0,D,c}(d)\), with the contrast remaining the headline.

\subsection{Bootstrap coverage on a subset}\label{sec:sim-boot}

Coverage of the season-year block bootstrap of Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-inference} is more expensive, since it refits all methods inside every resample, and is assessed on a smaller subset of cells if feasible. In a subset replication \(r\), fields are simulated and fitted, \(B_{\mathrm{boot}}\) season-year resamples produce pointwise \(95\%\) percentile intervals \(CI_{M,r}^{0.95}(d)\) for \(\Delta_M^{\chi}(d)\), and the empirical coverage is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sim-cov}
  \operatorname{Coverage}_{D,M}(d)
  =\frac1{R_{\mathrm{boot}}}\sum_{r=1}^{R_{\mathrm{boot}}}
  \mathbf 1\bigl\{\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(d)\in CI_{M,r}^{0.95}(d)\bigr\},
\end{equation}
with target \(0.95\). Because the simulated fields are independent across days, the bootstrap is exercised without within-block serial dependence, so \eqref{eq:sim-cov} is a best case: it confirms calibration absent serial dependence, whereas on the data the season-year block is the device meant to cope with the within-storm dependence the simulation does not contain (Sect.~\ref{sec:sim-interp}).

\subsection{Monte Carlo algorithm}\label{sec:sim-alg}

\begin{algorithm}[t]
  \caption{Three-method Monte Carlo benchmark (one DGP at a time)}
  \label{alg:sim-mc}
  \begin{algorithmic}[1]
    \For{each DGP \(D\in\{\mathrm{BR},\mathrm{ExtT},\mathrm{tCop}\}\)}
    \For{each scenario \(s\in\{H_0^{\mathrm{sim}},H_1^{\mathrm{sim}}\}\)}
    \State fix the DGP parameters; record \(\chi_{0,D,c}(\cdot)\) and \(\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}(\cdot)\) on \(\mathcal D\);
    \For{\(r=1,\dots,R\) (reduced \(R\) for the tCop DGP if required)}
    \State 1. simulate winter and summer fields on \(\{s_i\}_{i=1}^N\) from \(D\) (native scale);
    \State 2. fit every method \(M\in\{\mathrm{BR},\mathrm{ExtT},\mathrm{tCop}\}\) (Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-cl});
    \State 3. compute the fitted curves \(\widehat\chi_{M,c,r}^{(D)}(d_g)\) on \(\mathcal D\);
    \State 4. compute the differences \(\widehat\Delta_{M,r}^{\chi,(D)}(d_g)=\widehat\chi_{M,\mathrm W,r}^{(D)}(d_g)-\widehat\chi_{M,\mathrm S,r}^{(D)}(d_g)\);
    \State 5. store parameter estimates (diagonal), convergence flags, boundary flags, and computation time;
    \EndFor
    \State aggregate \eqref{eq:sim-bias}--\eqref{eq:sim-bound} over \(r\) for every \(M\);
    \EndFor
    \EndFor
    \State on the chosen subset, run the block bootstrap and aggregate the coverage \eqref{eq:sim-cov}.
  \end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

The outputs are organised around \(\chi(d)\) and \(\Delta^{\chi}(d)\), never \(\rho\): the filled Table~\ref{tab:sim-scenarios} with the induced contrast on \(\mathcal D_0\); \(\operatorname{Bias}_{D,M}(d)\), \(\operatorname{RMSE}_{D,M}(d)\), and \(\operatorname{MISE}_{D,M}\) over the \((D,M)\) grid; \(\operatorname{OR}_{D,M}(d)\) and \(\operatorname{FP}_{D,M}(d;\varepsilon)\), with interval-exclusion rates where available; \(\operatorname{Conv}_{D,M}\), \(\operatorname{Bound}_{D,M}\) per coordinate, and timing; figures overlaying \(\chi_{0,D,c}(d)\) with the fitted \(\widehat\chi_{M,c}^{(D)}(d)\) (mean and a Monte Carlo band) for representative cells; boxplots of \(\widehat\Delta_M^{\chi,(D)}(d)\) at \(\mathcal D_0\) with \(\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}\) marked; and, where run, \(\operatorname{Coverage}_{D,M}(d)\) on \(\mathcal D_0\). No entries are filled until the experiments are run.

\subsection{Scope and limitations}\label{sec:sim-interp}

The benchmark supports three readings. On the diagonal, a method is supported if it shows small bias and RMSE for the contrast on \(\mathcal D_0\), small \(\operatorname{MISE}\), ordering recovery near one under \(H_1^{\mathrm{sim}}\), a controlled spurious-ordering rate under \(H_0^{\mathrm{sim}}\), and dependable numerics. Off the diagonal, the experiment shows how far a fitted contrast departs from the true contrast under a wrong model, and in particular whether the contrast --- where a shared bias in both seasonal curves partly cancels --- is more robust than the levels. Reading the three methods side by side on data with a known \(\Delta_{0,D}^{\chi}\) calibrates the agreement rule of Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-comparison}: how often the methods agree on a genuinely positive contrast, and how often they spuriously agree at the null.

Two limits bound every reading. The range-type parameters are estimated less precisely than the shape, and for the \(t\)-based methods \((\nu,\rho)\) are weakly jointly identified when the data are uninformative about the floor, which the per-coordinate boundary rates make visible and which is a further reason to compare on \(\chi(d)\). And the study uses independent replicated fields with known margins, so it speaks to recovery of the seasonal contrast and to coverage absent serial dependence; it does not assess within-storm serial dependence or marginal estimation, both present in the application and the reason for, respectively, the season-year block bootstrap and the in-bootstrap margin re-estimation of Sect.~\ref{sec:meth-inference}. Within this scope, the simulation is designed to establish whether the three procedures recover a seasonal tail-dependence difference that is present, refrain from manufacturing one that is absent, and agree to the degree the empirical comparison relies on; it does not establish that any DGP is the true mechanism of the KNMI wind-gust fields.
