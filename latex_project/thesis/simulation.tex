\section{Simulation design (optional)}\label{sec:simulation}

This section describes a small simulation that is intended as a
methodological validation exercise for the empirical pipeline of
Section~\ref{sec:methodology}. The purpose is not to introduce a
new statistical model; it is to check that the pairwise extremal
dependence estimator and the dependence--distance smoother can
recover a seasonal contrast that is built into the data-generating
process by construction. The simulation is optional and produces
no empirical claim about Dutch wind gusts.

\subsection{Purpose}\label{sec:sim-purpose}

We consider whether the proposed pipeline can detect that one
seasonal regime has stronger spatial extremal dependence than the
other when this difference is known to be present. Concretely we
simulate two regimes that differ only in their spatial dependence
range, apply the estimator of
Section~\ref{sec:meth-dependence}, and compare the estimated
dependence--distance curves with their analytic counterparts.

\subsection{Synthetic station locations}\label{sec:sim-locations}

We consider \(N^{\mathrm{sim}}\) synthetic station locations
\(s_i^{\mathrm{sim}} \in [0, L] \times [0, L]\) sampled uniformly
on a square domain of side length \(L\) kilometres. Both
\(N^{\mathrm{sim}}\) and \(L\) are chosen of the same order of
magnitude as the Dutch KNMI panel:
\(N^{\mathrm{sim}} \approx 30\), \(L \approx 300\) km.
The distance matrix \(\{d_{ij}^{\mathrm{sim}}\}\) is the matrix of
pairwise Euclidean distances.

\subsection{Two seasonal dependence
regimes}\label{sec:sim-regimes}

We simulate two regimes, indexed by \(c \in \{\mathrm{W},\mathrm{S}\}\),
that share the same station locations and differ only in a single
dependence-range parameter \(\rho^{(c)}\). The winter regime has
\(\rho^{(\mathrm{W})} > \rho^{(\mathrm{S})}\), so that under the
construction \(\chi^{(\mathrm{W})}(d) > \chi^{(\mathrm{S})}(d)\) at
every distance \(d\) of interest.

\subsection{Simulated extreme observations}\label{sec:sim-data}

We generate \(B^{\mathrm{sim}}\) independent replications per
regime, each replication consisting of one block-maximum
observation per synthetic station. The simulated panel is
therefore an \(N^{\mathrm{sim}} \times B^{\mathrm{sim}}\) matrix
per regime. We do not simulate sub-block hourly observations; the
purpose is to test the pair-level estimator on block maxima, which
is the headline route of Section~\ref{sec:meth-extremes}.

\subsection{Estimator}\label{sec:sim-estimator}

The same estimator as in the empirical chapter is applied to the
simulated panel:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Marginal rank transform per station per regime.
    \item Empirical \(\widehat\chi_{ij}^{(c)}(u_0)\) at a fixed
          reference level \(u_0\) for every pair.
    \item Empirical \(\widehat\theta_{ij}^{(c)}\) via the
          F-madogram on block maxima.
    \item Local-linear kernel smoother
          \(\widehat\chi^{(c)}(d)\) over distance.
\end{enumerate}

The estimator is implemented in
\texttt{python\_project/src/dependence\_estimators.py} for the
empirical pipeline and is reused without modification in the
simulation. The simulation driver is
\texttt{python\_project/src/simulation.py}, called from
\texttt{notebooks/04\_simulation\_design.ipynb}.

\subsection{Evaluation metrics}\label{sec:sim-metrics}

We evaluate three things.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{Pair-level bias.} Mean and standard deviation of
          \(\widehat\chi_{ij}^{(c)}(u_0) - \chi_{ij}^{(c)}(u_0)\)
          across pairs and replications.
    \item \emph{Recovery of the seasonal ordering.} Fraction of
          replications in which the smoothed curve
          \(\widehat\chi^{(\mathrm{W})}(d)\) is uniformly above
          \(\widehat\chi^{(\mathrm{S})}(d)\) on the reference
          distance grid.
    \item \emph{Coverage.} Empirical coverage of the block-
          bootstrap percentile interval for
          \(\widehat\chi^{(c)}(d)\) at the reference distances.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Expected interpretation}\label{sec:sim-interpretation}

A successful simulation produces approximately unbiased pair-level
estimates, recovers the seasonal ordering in the large majority of
replications, and shows bootstrap coverage close to the nominal
level. If any of these three diagnostics fails, the empirical
estimates of Section~\ref{sec:results} should be re-interpreted in
light of the failure mode (e.g.\ systematic underestimation of
\(\chi\), or under-coverage of the bootstrap band).

\subsection{Scope and disclaimer}\label{sec:sim-disclaimer}

The simulation is by construction stationary, isotropic and based
on a known dependence family. It is not a model of Dutch wind
gusts. Its role is to certify that the estimator behaves as
expected when its assumptions are met, so that the seasonal
contrast in the empirical chapter can be more confidently
attributed to the data rather than to estimator artefacts. This
chapter contains no empirical claim about Dutch wind gusts.
