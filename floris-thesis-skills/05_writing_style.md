Preferred thesis structure:

1. Introduction
2. Data
3. Extreme value theory and spatial extremal dependence
4. Methodology
5. Empirical design
6. Results
7. Robustness checks
8. Conclusion
Appendices if needed

Write academically, but not overly abstractly.
Prefer clear econometric motivation over climate-policy storytelling.
Always connect methods to the research question.
Avoid making the thesis sound like a pure meteorology thesis.
When possible, explain intuition before notation.
Every section should have a clear purpose.
Avoid long literature summaries that are not connected to the method.
Use notation only after giving intuition.
Do not overclaim. Use careful language such as "evidence suggests" rather than "proves".
